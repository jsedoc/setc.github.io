Neural Conversational Mode (NCM)

In (Vinyals and Le 2015), human evaluation is conducted on a set of 200 hand-picked prompts. We make this dataset available for comparison.

Dialogue Breakdown Detection (DBDC)

The Dialogue Breakdown Detection (DBDC) task is part of the Dialogue System Technology Challenge, a series of tasks for understanding human dialog. The DBDC dataset consists of a series of text-based conversations between a human and a chatbot where human was aware they were chatting with a computer. The intended goal of the task was to predict at what turn in the conversation a breakdown in communication starts to occur. We instead use this dataset as a reasonable example of how humans communicate with chatbots. We randomly extract 200 human utterances from the conversations.


Baselines

OpenNMT Seq2Seq Twitter

OpenNMT Seq2Seq with its default parameters, trained by the SETC team on Twitter.

OpenNMT Seq2Seq OpenSubtitles

OpenNMT Seq2Seq with its default parameters, trained by the SETC team on OpenSubtitles.

Cakechat

We used the pre-trained model weights released for Cakechat by Lukalabs. The model was trained on pre-processed conversational data from Twitter and was designed to generate responses biased by a specified emotion.

Neural Conversational Model (NCM)

This baseline consists of the responses reported by (Vinyals and Le 2015) on their evaluation dataset. It only exists for this dataset.

Human

For the Random Twitter and Random OpenSubtitles datasets, this baseline consists of the responses from the dataset itself. For NCM, this baseline consists responses generated by the SETC team. There is no human baseline for the DBDC dataset.
